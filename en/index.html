Philosophy

It reframes financial clarity in language closer to life.

What affects emotion is rarely a balance by itself. It is what that balance means. Runway is built to answer three questions quickly: where you stand now, whether the recent direction is improving or worsening, and what deserves your attention next.

Time is not decorative language here. It is the clearest expression of buffer, optionality, and room to breathe.

01 · See the present

Assets, cash flow, and baseline spending belong in one frame.

Instead of splitting finance into isolated modules, Runway pulls the numbers back into a picture you can actually read as a whole.

02 · Explain direction

Translate change into runway, buffer, and pace rather than more chart noise.

Trends should answer what the movement means, not leave you alone with more lines to interpret.

03 · Stay truthful

Separate facts, estimates, and assumptions instead of hiding uncertainty behind fake precision.

When the data is incomplete, the product should explain its limits instead of pretending it knows more.

Trust

Privacy is not a footer note. It is part of the product structure.

Financial data is deeply personal. Runway is shaped around a local-first mindset, keeping core records and calculations on device whenever possible. If future versions introduce cloud sync, accounts, or remote backup, that change will be stated clearly before it ships.

Local-first Core records belong on your device first.

Tracking, trends, and projections should work locally before sensitive information is treated as something to upload by default.

Traceable Key metrics need provenance and boundaries.

If a number comes from real records, a manual baseline, or a scenario assumption, the product should say so plainly.

Reachable Support and privacy contact should stay easy to find.

If you need help with privacy, support, or data handling, you should be able to reach Fuhai Lab directly.
